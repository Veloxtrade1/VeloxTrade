Trading Glossary

Key terms every trader needs to know.

Pip
The smallest price movement in a currency pair. For most pairs, 1 pip = 0.0001 (0.01 for JPY pairs).
Spread
The difference between the buy (ask) and sell (bid) price. This is the broker's fee for executing trades.
Leverage
Allows trading larger positions than your actual capital. 1:100 leverage means $100 controls $10,000 in trades.
Margin
The minimum amount required to open and maintain a leveraged trading position.
Stop Loss
An order that automatically closes a trade when price reaches a specified level to limit losses.
Take Profit
An order that automatically closes a trade when price reaches a target profit level.
Long Position
Buying an asset expecting its price to rise. You profit when price increases from your entry point.
Short Position
Selling an asset expecting its price to fall. You profit when price decreases from your entry point.
Support Level
A price level where buying pressure has historically prevented further price decline.
Resistance Level
A price level where selling pressure has historically prevented further price increases.
Lot
The standard unit of trading. 1 standard lot = 100,000 units. Mini lot = 10,000. Micro lot = 1,000 units.
RSI (Relative Strength Index)
A momentum indicator measuring speed and change of price movements. Values above 70 = overbought, below 30 = oversold.
